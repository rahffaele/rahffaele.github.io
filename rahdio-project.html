Rahdio - A city music synthesizer

2023

This page is WIP

RAHDIO is a personal exercise/project i developed to investigate how generative algorithms could be used to represent data.

Inspired by Brian Eno's work, it takes live data from cities around the globe and translates them into infinite, generative soundscapes.

The algorithm takes into account city-specific data such as temperature, traffic/human presence, air pollution, time, wind speed and weather to synthesize sound in-browser, along with randomized factors and a technique heavily inspired by Brian Eno's album Discreet Music.

On the UI side, i tried to design a clean, data-focused interface, to make clear what's influencing the sound produced. It works mimicking a music app where the user can choose a track from a playlist - in this case a list of cities. The background gradient is defined by pollution, temperature and changes over time. The user can tweak the tracks volume parameters, the heaviness of the delay effect and is given the option to play ambient noise tracks that are only available under defined weather conditions of the selected city.
